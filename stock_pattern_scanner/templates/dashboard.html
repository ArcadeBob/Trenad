Investor Guide

What Is This App?

This scanner finds stocks forming base patterns — specific price consolidation shapes that often come before major price moves. It's based on the CAN SLIM methodology developed by William O'Neil in How to Make Money in Stocks.

The app scans stocks, scores how closely each pattern matches the ideal textbook formation, and shows you which ones are closest to a buy point — the price level where a breakout is expected.

How to Use the Scanner

  1. Pick a watchlist — Growth for curated picks, S&P 500 or NASDAQ 100 for broader scans
  2. Set a minimum score — start with 50+ to see quality patterns
  3. Click Scan and wait for results
  4. Focus on "At Pivot" and "Near Pivot" stocks with scores of 75+
  5. Check quality filters — RS above 80, Vol Rating A or B, above both 50MA and 200MA
  6. Use the buy point as your entry price, set a stop loss 7% below

Pattern Guide

Cup & Handle

A U-shaped consolidation (the "cup") followed by a small pullback (the "handle"). The buy point is at the top of the cup. This is the most common and reliable pattern.

Ideal depth: 12-33% | Duration: 7-65 weeks | Look for volume dry-up in the handle

Deep Cup & Handle

Same shape as Cup & Handle but with a deeper pullback (33-50%). These form in volatile or bear markets and carry more risk, but can produce large gains when they work.

Depth: 33-50% | Duration: 7-65 weeks | Higher risk, higher reward

Double Bottom

A W-shaped pattern with two distinct lows near the same price level. The buy point is at the middle peak of the W. Shows the stock found strong price support twice — a bullish signal.

Depth: 15-40% | Two lows within 5% of each other | Buy at the middle peak

Flat Base

A tight sideways consolidation where the stock trades in a very narrow range (less than 15%). Usually forms after a prior advance. The tightness shows institutions are quietly accumulating shares without driving the price down.

Max depth: 15% | Duration: 5-15 weeks | Volume should contract during base

Reading Your Results

After a scan completes, focus on these key areas:

  • Score 75+ = high-quality pattern that closely matches the ideal formation
  • "At Pivot" status = the stock is at its buy point, potentially actionable now
  • RS 80+ = stock is outperforming most of the market (institutional demand)
  • Vol Rating A or B = institutions are accumulating (buying) the stock
  • Above 50MA and 200MA = both short-term and long-term trends are healthy
  • Volume confirmed (✓) = the breakout had strong volume support

The buy point is your entry price. Set your stop loss 7% below it to protect against failed breakouts. Your profit target of 20% above gives a healthy 3:1 risk/reward ratio.

Understanding the Backtest

The backtest replays 2 years of historical data, detects patterns that formed in the past, and simulates what would have happened if you traded every signal using your stop loss and profit target settings.

Key metrics to watch:

  • Win Rate above 50% is good, but even 40% can be profitable if avg wins >> avg losses
  • Profit Factor above 1.5 suggests a solid strategy
  • Positive Expectancy means the strategy makes money over many trades

Use the breakdown tables to see which patterns and market conditions produce the best results. Most patterns perform much better during confirmed uptrends.

Past performance does not guarantee future results. Use backtests to understand strategy behavior, not to predict exact returns.

Glossary

Base Pattern
A period of price consolidation (sideways movement) after an advance. The stock is "resting" before potentially moving higher. Think of it as coiling a spring.
Buy Point / Pivot
The price level where a base pattern completes and a breakout is expected. This is the ideal entry price for buying the stock.
CAN SLIM
An investment strategy by William O'Neil focusing on Current earnings, Annual earnings, New products/management, Supply and demand, Leader/laggard, Institutional sponsorship, and Market direction.
Confidence Score
A 0-100 rating measuring how closely a detected pattern matches the ideal formation. Factors include depth, volume profile, trend strength, relative strength, and breakout quality.
Distribution Day
A day where the S&P 500 closes down more than 0.2% on volume higher than the previous day. Clusters of distribution days signal institutional selling and potential market corrections.
Expectancy
The average expected profit per trade, factoring in both win rate and the relative sizes of wins vs losses. Positive expectancy means the strategy is profitable over time.
Market Regime
The overall market condition: "Confirmed Uptrend" (healthy, good for buying), "Under Pressure" (caution), or "In Correction" (avoid new buys). Based on S&P 500 trend and distribution day count.
Moving Average (50/200-day)
The average closing price over the last 50 or 200 trading days. Acts as a trend indicator — price above = healthy trend, price below = weakening. The 200MA represents the long-term trend.
Profit Factor
Total gross profits divided by total gross losses. Above 1.0 = profitable, above 2.0 = strong strategy. Below 1.0 = losing money overall.
Relative Strength (RS)
A 1-99 rating comparing a stock's price performance to the S&P 500. An RS of 90 means the stock outperformed 90% of all stocks. Look for 80+ for leading stocks.
Stop Loss
A pre-set price where you'll sell if the trade goes against you. The default 7% rule limits any single loss to a manageable amount. Never hold through a broken stop loss.
Volume Confirmation
When a stock breaks out above its buy point with trading volume at least 40% above its average. This confirms institutional participation and increases breakout reliability.
Volume Rating (A-E)
Grades institutional activity: A = heavy buying (accumulation), B = moderate buying, C = neutral, D = moderate selling, E = heavy selling (distribution). Look for A or B.
Win Rate
The percentage of trades that ended profitably. A useful but incomplete metric — a 40% win rate can still be very profitable if winners are much larger than losers.
